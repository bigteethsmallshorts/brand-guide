Signature Device
Tape Label

How we put event-critical info on posters — dates, venues, deadlines, URLs. Stack with a slight tilt. The handmade feel is the point.

Thursday Feb 05, 2026 Facets Cinema Chicago, IL
How to Make a Tape Label
  • Use the tape PNG asset as your background
  • Text: FatFrank Heavy, all-caps, centered, navy
  • Rotate slightly — 1 to 3 degrees either direction
  • Stack multiple labels with a small offset
  • For: dates, venues, URLs, deadlines only

Photo Mosaic / Collage

Grid of film stills or audience photos on a navy or slate background. Works well for carousels and between-show recap posts. No borders between images — let them butt up tight or use a thin navy gap. Feels editorial, not promotional.

Photography
Photo Use

Audience photos are the primary asset. Film stills are secondary. Use them to show the experience, not just promote the product.

Which Photo for Which Format
Wide landscape Banners, email headers, website hero — leave left-side space for type
Square close-up Instagram posts, film cards — faces readable at small size
Film stills Winner announcements, selection notifications, booklet only
Photo Tips
  • Choose photos where faces show genuine reaction
  • Leave compositional room for type — don't crop too tight
  • Credit photographers in email footer when used
  • Desaturate fully before applying b&w treatment
Avoid
  • Warm tints — always cool (navy/slate)
  • Type directly on unmodified photo without overlay
  • Film stills as banner backgrounds — too busy
Photo Overlays (Banners + Email Headers)
  • ·Full-bleed photo, darkened with a cool overlay, type and illustration on top
  • ·Navy overlay at 55–80% opacity — always dark enough for white type
  • ·Tint is always cool — navy or slate, never warm
  • ·Illustration anchored to one edge, type on the opposite side
  • ·One accent color element max — rose pill OR denim date line, not both
