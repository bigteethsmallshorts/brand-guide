Accent Palette

Used exclusively when celebrating a specific film or filmmaker — Official Selection announcements, winner posts, award callouts. One color per post, rotated across the set. Never in print. Never on the primary logo mark. Never mixed in the same post.

The rule: If a post celebrates a film or filmmaker, use the accent palette. If it's operational — deadlines, tickets, carousels, headers — stay in the primary palette. See page 4 for approved background + laurel combinations.

Big Teeth Small Shorts Film Fest · Brand Guidelines 2026 Social Formats · 3 of 4 · Internal Brand Reference · 2026 Edition

These formats represent our current working system. Layouts evolve season to season — the consistent elements are the typography, color palette, and brand devices, not the exact composition.

Official Selection

Accent palette. Header bar in primary bg + accent laurel. Film still + FatFrank title. See page 4 for approved color combos.

Production: InDesign + Data Merge. Title, filmmaker, still swap per spreadsheet record. One export = full selection set.

Deadline Post

Primary palette only. Split composition — FatFrank title left, illustration right. Rose pill for deadline name. Tape label for URL.

Note: Current versions being updated for Instagram safe zones.

Deadline · Photo Variant

Primary palette only. Same split system — audience photos replace illustration. Works well for later deadlines when energy needs a boost.

Ticket Launch · 50% Off

Primary palette only. Slate bg, photo grid, rose CTA block. 50% off window is 24 hours — post goes out the moment tickets go live.

Carousel · Collage

Primary palette only. Slate bg · film stills + audience photos butted tight · FatFrank headlines · tape labels. Used for ticket launch and between-show recaps.

Email Banner · Event Header

Primary palette only. Audience photo behind · cool dark overlay · FatFrank title left · shorts illustration right · event info inline. This is the email banner format — same layout adapts to social header, web hero, and partner listings with crop and safe zone adjustments per format.

Always
  • Shorts illustration as your primary logo
  • Navy as your default dark background
  • FatFrank for primary headlines — Antarctican when narrower treatment needed
  • Tablet Gothic or DM Sans for body copy
  • Rotate all four Accent Palette colors across Official Selection posts
  • Stack tape labels with a slight tilt
  • "Presented By Big Teeth Productions" on all event materials
Never
  • Recolor the shorts illustration
  • Accent Palette colors in print materials
  • FatFrank for body copy
  • Rounded corners on anything
  • BTP logo prominent — footer credit only
  • Warm tints on photos — always cool (navy/slate overlay)
  • More than one Accent Palette color in the same post
